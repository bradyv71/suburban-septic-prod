Why Inspecting the Septic Tank is Essential Before Buying a House

Intro:

Before finalizing the purchase of a new house, it is crucial to prioritize a professional septic tank inspection. Simply assuming that everything is in order down there because the house seems perfect can lead to costly surprises down the line. By investing a little time and money in a professional's evaluation of the health of your drainpipes and septic system, such as the experts at our Suburban Septic Service, you can avoid potential septic tank problems and make informed decisions during the negotiation process.

Why Trust a Professional Inspection:

When it comes to assessing the condition of your septic system, relying on the seller's disclosure may not provide the full picture. Even if they are aware of any issues, they might not disclose them willingly. By obtaining maintenance and cleaning records, as well as inquiring about the installation date of the septic tank, you can gain valuable insights. If the tank has been in use for 25 or more years, it may require replacement in the near future.

Visual Inspections for Red Flags:

You don't have to be an expert to notice certain warning signs in the backyard and drain field. Check for standing water over the septic tank in an otherwise dry area, as it could indicate a problem. Unpleasant odors emanating from the backyard might also be a telltale sign of septic system issues. As the saying goes, "The grass grows greenest over the septic tank," particularly when it requires draining or is experiencing leaks. Inside the house, flushing the toilet a few times and listening for slow drainage or gurgling sounds can provide further clues.

Professional Inspection Methods:

Considering a septic tank inspection as an unnecessary expense when buying a house would be a mistake. At Suburban Septic Service, our experienced plumbers routinely conduct these inspections for buyers, sellers, and real estate agents. In fact, real estate agents or sellers who are motivated to sell may even contribute to the inspection costs. By employing fiber-optic camera technology, we can visually assess your drainpipes and tank, detecting potential issues such as tree root intrusion or damage.

Conclusion:

Before you purchase a house, investing in a professional septic tank inspection is an essential step that can save you from future headaches and unexpected expenses. By seeking expert advice, conducting visual inspections, and utilizing dye water testing, you can gain a comprehensive understanding of the septic system's condition. Trusting the expertise of professionals like those at Suburban Septic Service ensures you enter the real estate transaction with confidence and knowledge.
